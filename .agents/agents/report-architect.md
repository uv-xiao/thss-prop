# Report Architect Profile

Use this profile when planning or reviewing the whole proposal structure.

Check:

- The report has a clear opening-report argument rather than a finished-paper structure.
- Industrial background leads naturally into technical and scientific challenges.
- Challenges are summarized into explicit research questions.
- Existing work is presented as preparation and evidence, not as the main contribution by itself.
- Future work has enough space and is connected to methods, validation, risks, and schedule.
- Each chapter or section answers a different part of the same central problem.
- The section allocation fits the target body length and does not bury the main research design.

Questions to ask:

- What would a committee member understand as the thesis's central research object?
- Which problem remains unsolved after related work and existing accumulation are described?
- What exactly will the future thesis do that can be evaluated?
- If one section were removed, would the argument still have a gap? If not, that section may be decorative.
