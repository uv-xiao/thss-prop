# Chinese Academic Style Rules

These rules adapt Chinese mixed-language style guidance and Tsinghua thesis writing expectations for this report.

## Language Register

- Use simplified Chinese and Mainland Chinese academic terminology.
- Use formal academic prose. Do not use internet slang, promotional language, slogan-like phrasing, or conversational fillers.
- Prefer direct, concrete sentences over ornate prose.
- Avoid empty intensifiers such as “非常重要”“极大地”“革命性”“深刻地改变” unless the sentence provides evidence and a precise object.
- Avoid long noun stacks translated from English. If a term becomes hard to parse, split the sentence or define the term.

## Chinese and English Mixing

- Add a space between Chinese characters and English words, abbreviations, Arabic numerals, and inline code.
- Do not add spaces between Chinese text and Chinese punctuation.
- Use Chinese punctuation in Chinese sentences.
- Use English punctuation inside standalone English clauses or quoted English source text.
- Use the singular form for English nouns embedded in Chinese unless quoting or naming a source exactly.
- For a term whose Chinese translation is not widely accepted or may be ambiguous, use the Chinese term followed by the English original at first appearance, then use the chosen Chinese term consistently.
- Do not repeatedly parenthesize the same English term after it has been introduced.

## Punctuation and Lists

- Use “，”“。 ”“；”“：”“（ ）”“《 》”““ ”” in Chinese text according to normal Chinese punctuation rules.
- Use “、” for parallel Chinese words or short phrases. Do not use an English-style serial comma before “和” or “或”.
- Use “/” only when alternatives are genuinely interchangeable or paired labels are conventional.
- Use “——” sparingly. Prefer explicit causal or contrastive wording in academic argument.
- In list items that are not full sentences, use semicolons except the final item, which ends with a full stop. In full-sentence lists, every item ends with a full stop.

## Numbers and Units

- Use Arabic numerals for 10 and above, measurements, dates, version numbers, code metrics, and percentages.
- Use Chinese numerals for one to nine when they are ordinary count words and not technical measurements.
- Use half-width `%`, mathematical symbols, and SI units.
- Use a space between numbers and Chinese units when mixed with English or technical notation where readability requires it; keep conventional Chinese expressions natural.

## Terminology

- Maintain the project terminology table in `docs/writing/terminology.md`.
- Do not invent a Chinese translation for a technical term without checking existing usage in sources, code, or prior report text.
- If a term appears in English source material, preserve the source phrase in the terminology table with context and source path or citation key.
- The terminology table is a writing aid and must not be automatically inserted into the report.

## Tsinghua Writing Expectations

- The report should use correct wording, smooth language, reliable data, clear expression, and standardized figures, tables, formulas, and units.
- Non-public or specialized concepts must be explained when introduced.
- The title should be clear, point to the research object, and avoid unnecessary breadth.
