# Writing Preferences

This file records project-specific preferences that are important but less universal than `.agents/rules/`.

## Current Preferences

- The report must satisfy the Integrated Circuit School doctoral topic-report expectations for microelectronics and solid-state electronics / integrated circuit science and engineering: research purpose and topic significance, key scientific questions, innovation, research methods, research content and staged results, research plan, and references must all be present and reviewable.
- Treat written-report quality and oral-defense readiness as linked. The prose should prepare for committee questions about topic selection, literature survey depth, innovation and practical value, research feasibility, foundational/specialized knowledge, scientific reasoning, academic norms, logic, and language expression.
- During serious report-quality improvement, use the project-local `grill-me` skill repeatedly to align on one writing or research-design decision at a time.
- The report theme is “敏捷芯片设计与创新性编译技术驱动的软硬件协同”. Do not reframe it as AI education, intelligent programming education, or a narrow C/system programming learning system.
- The central relation should be cross-architecture hardware-software co-design ecology. Compilation and architecture are the two key interface layers that connect software demand, hardware capability, synthesis optimization, verification evidence, system software boundaries, and target execution environments.
- When describing the relationship between architecture and compilation, prefer "协同" over "双核心". Do not frame the target capability only as a "敏捷芯片设计体系", because that weakens the role of compilation support. The prose should present agile chip design and innovative compilation technology as mutually defining, constraining, validating, and evolving inside one integrated hardware-software co-design system.
- Use "发现、实现、证明/演化" as the hidden argument logic for the report's storyline, but do not turn these verbs into slogan-like chapter titles or formal scientific-question headings. Keep formal wording centered on architecture interfaces, high-quality hardware implementation, compilation interfaces, and validation-constrained evolution.
- Frame report-level innovation in three layers: interface-abstraction innovation, formal/compiler-method innovation, and system-capability innovation. Do not use a flat list of paper contributions as the innovation structure; papers and systems are evidence for these higher-level innovation claims.
- For each innovation layer, specify where it is introduced and where implementation is expanded. Keep the link to research methods clear, but do not over-emphasize "evidence validation" language; use implementation route, representative systems, experiments, and future plan where appropriate.
- In the introduction's related-work review, classify literature by co-design interfaces and problem transitions rather than by paper or technology object: application-to-architecture, architecture-to-hardware, architecture-constrained compilation optimization, and agentic methods.
- Keep the four Chapter 1.4 survey categories as the survey scaffold, write them as explicit classified subsections, and then converge them into the three core scientific questions in Chapter 1.5. Do not compress the literature survey into one short section or only three problem-oriented paragraphs, because that weakens literature breadth and interface-level analysis.
- Chapter 1.4's related-work subsections should be substantially detailed: each subsection must explain why that category exists, how the category is internally divided, what representative work families show, what source-paper related-work sections identify as limitations, and how the subsection contributes to the report's background or conclusion. The total discussion should be at least comparable to, and normally more detailed than, the combined related-work discussion in the source papers because the proposal literature review is not under the same page constraints as the original papers.
- Chapter 1.4 must survey external domestic/foreign research directly. Local existing papers under `resources/` are only literature-discovery seeds; the report正文 must not write "原文指出", "源文指出", or use the local papers themselves as the state-of-the-art subject in this section. Instead, inspect and summarize the external papers cited by those local works, classify them into a coherent survey logic, and cite the external work next to the discussed system or method.
- Before revising the introduction's related-work review, mine each original paper's introduction, background, motivation, and related-work sections, then update or consult `docs/writing/related-work-synthesis.md` and `docs/writing/related-work-bibliography.md`. The report prose should integrate those findings as a coherent survey, not as per-paper summaries.
- Before the next substantive Chapter 1.4 rewrite, run a per-source coverage audit using `docs/writing/related-work-coverage-audit-2026-05-11.md`: for each original paper, extract its survey/related-work categories, check whether the report discusses, analyzes, and classifies them, then turn gaps into concrete revision actions.
- Do not let the current introduction decide the bibliography boundary. The correct order is: first build a broad related-work reservoir from every source paper's citation network, then organize that reservoir into a more complete survey logic, then promote only the entries actually interpreted by the report into `report/refs.bib`.
- `docs/writing/related-work-bibliography.md` is the broad working index for related work. It should list source-paper citation keys, local BibTeX provenance, unresolved keys, and thematic grouping before Chapter 1 decides what to cite.
- `docs/writing/related-work-synthesis.md` must synthesize the reservoir into argument lines and bibliography routing. It should distinguish broad candidate references from entries already promoted into `report/refs.bib`, and Chapter 1 should cite direct related work when interpreting the survey.
- Keep the introduction's four survey categories in this order: application-to-architecture, architecture-to-hardware, architecture-constrained compilation optimization, and agentic methods. Do not keep a separate compilation-to-runtime category in report-facing prose; fold PyTorch/Dynamo, TensorRT-LLM, NVL72, CloudMatrix384, and UB-Mesh evidence into architecture-constrained compilation optimization.
- Do not add a core-question mapping table in Chapter 1.5. Use continuous academic narrative for problem convergence; use the Chapter 1.6 structure figure for mapping.
- The introduction should converge to exactly three core research questions, not four or five. The four survey categories are for literature organization; the three questions are the report's research-problem scaffold.
- At the end of the introduction, map chapters to research questions using “primary question + secondary support”. Avoid rigid one-to-one mapping, but keep each chapter's main role explicit.
- Do not add an independent summary chapter for staged results or existing work. Use Chapter 1.6 as a concise roadmap that maps existing work to research questions and feasibility, then let Chapters 2-4 provide the detailed staged-result evidence.
- In the introduction, delay the user's own completed work until section 1.6. Sections 1.1-1.4 should build the field review and problem chain first, without turning into an achievement summary.
- Chapter 1 needs industry data and recent top architecture-conference evidence, not only qualitative claims. Use `docs/writing/introduction-evidence-plan.md` before drafting the introduction.
- Prefer introduction evidence from large-company authors, industrial research labs, authoritative experts, industrial system papers, official technical reports, and top architecture/systems conferences.
- Introduce evidence through representative examples at key argument transitions. Evidence tables are optional and should only be included if they improve verifiability without making the introduction feel like a technical report.
- For heterogeneous architecture complexity and programming concerns, prefer ATLAS/DeepStack, Huawei CloudMatrix384/UB-Mesh, and NVIDIA GB200 NVL72/IMEX over ISCA AIO or MICRO SCAR.
- For architecture-constrained compilation optimization, use PyTorch 2 `torch.compile`/TorchDynamo and NVIDIA TensorRT-LLM as primary framework evidence for dynamic program capture, backend compilation, kernel fusion, cache/batch/layout decisions, and system software boundaries; use NVL72/CloudMatrix/UB-Mesh as hardware-system context rather than as standalone framework evidence.

Inference can be the main system-software example and training/general dynamic programs can be supplements, but they should support the argument that compilation optimization is constrained by target architecture, communication topology, data movement, service backend, performance profiling, and execution traces.
- In Chapter 1.1, use AI/LLM as the main application pressure, then use PQC, DSP, point cloud, graphics, and other domains as evidence of domain-specialization diversity. Do not make the report appear to be only about LLM serving.
- Introduce “敏捷芯片设计” in Chapter 1.2 after explaining design-productivity and iteration bottlenecks. Define it as rapid formation, verification, mapping, and iteration of domain hardware capabilities, not merely faster RTL writing.
- Introduce LLM/agentic methods as a technology innovation and industry trend in Chapter 1, not only as a later future-work mechanism. Use a three-step placement: 1.1 briefly presents LLM/agentic as changing complex engineering automation; 1.2 connects it to chip-design productivity and AI-assisted design; 1.3 constrains it through architecture interfaces, compiler infrastructure, semantic gates, performance profiling, execution traces, and evidence memory.

Separate evidence types: AVO supports agentic kernel/compiler optimization on industrial GPUs; AlphaEvolve supports evaluator-driven evolutionary coding agents for algorithmic discovery; OpenAI/Anthropic support agentic coding systems, workflow integration, human oversight, and safety boundaries; Siemens/Synopsys support EDA vendor trend; METR and agentic software/EDA/compiler papers support research capability and risk; none alone proves complete autonomous chip design.

- Make the explicit Chapter 1 judgment: the core of agentic methods is not only the model, but the collaboration among model, tools, target constraints, execution feedback, and evidence memory. For chip design and compilation, this mechanism must be embedded in architecture interfaces, compiler IR, formal semantics, performance profiling, execution traces, and hardware verification.

- In Chapter 1.3 and the transition into Chapter 1.5, use `resources/compiler-infra-is-harness-medium.md` to frame compiler infrastructure as an agent harness medium: target facts, explicit IR, semantic contracts, constrained transformations, executable backend artifacts, gates, and evidence/feedback. Do not present this as free-form LLM compiler generation.

- In technical chapters, add more algorithms, formulas, recurrence relations, cost models, and problem formulations from the original papers when they clarify the method. In experimental subsections, prefer concise prose that lists the main result data and links each result back to the mechanism; avoid citing many experimental images.
- In section and subsection titles, use Chinese-first academic titles. Avoid tool names, English abbreviations, and dense specialized terms in headings unless the term is the topic itself and removing it would obscure the section. Put system names and exact English terms in the first paragraph instead.
- In body prose, translate ordinary English technical words and multi-word phrases into Chinese according to the terminology table. Retain English for names such as APS/Aquas/ISAMORE/Cayman/Cement/Clay/SkyEgg/EggMind, standard abbreviations such as RTL/HLS/MLIR/LLM/ASIP/ISAX, code identifiers, exact package names, and first-use parenthetical forms needed for source alignment.

- When an original source presents content as mathematical notation, preserve it as mathematical notation in the report, including formulas inside tables, captions, problem formulations, recurrences, constraints, cost models, complexity expressions, and result transformations. Do not downgrade source formulas to backtick code, prose-only paraphrases, or plain text table cells unless the source itself uses code syntax rather than mathematics.
- When using a table from an original paper or local source, preserve the source table's layout logic: row/column grouping, multi-level headers, row spans, column spans, notes, metric ordering, visual markers, and math/code representation. Use advanced Typst table features such as `table.cell(rowspan:)`, `table.cell(colspan:)`, compact text, proportional column widths, captions/notes, and color markers rather than collapsing the table into a simplified prose-style summary.

After compiling, render and visually inspect every table page; if a source layout is cramped, illegible, or visually awkward in the single-column report, re-layout it while preserving the source's technical fields, formulas, row semantics, and captioned provenance.
- For single-column report layout, size figures by aspect ratio and information density rather than applying one default width. Wide workflow figures may use near full width; square or tall mechanism figures should be reduced enough to preserve surrounding prose and avoid oversized pages.
- Use `docs/writing/terminology-decisions.md` as the review workspace for terms whose Chinese/English treatment is still undecided. Do not bulk rewrite unsettled terms in the report before the user has reviewed the table.
- The introduction should include two newly drawn overview figures: “跨硬件架构软硬件协同生态” and “研究问题与章节映射”.
- The writing should be dense, logical, and restrained. Avoid long generic introductions.
- Translation should preserve the English source's technical modality and scope.
- The report should not include the terminology table as a visible section unless explicitly requested.

## Preferred Argument Moves

- Start from a concrete industrial or engineering pressure, then narrow to a research challenge.
- Turn a challenge into a question before proposing a method.
- Use comparisons to explain why the proposed method is targeted.
- Use existing systems as evidence of feasibility and accumulated insight.
- Make future plans measurable through artifacts, experiments, case studies, or evaluation criteria.

## Phrases To Avoid Unless Evidence Supports Them

- “具有重要意义”
- “显著提升”
- “彻底解决”
- “首次提出”
- “完整证明”
- “革命性改变”
